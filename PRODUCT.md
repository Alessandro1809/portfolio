# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Primary:** Technical leads, CTOs, and senior engineers who will read project descriptions with the intent of verifying whether the stack choices were sound, whether the architecture was Alessandro's own call, and whether the communication style fits a high-trust working relationship.

**Secondary:** Founders and product managers who care about shipping track record, judgment under ambiguity, and whether Alessandro can own a feature end-to-end without hand-holding.

Both audiences arrive from LinkedIn, GitHub, or a referral. They spend 8-15 seconds on first load, then either leave or read. The portfolio must pass the visual scan (is this person credible?) before any word is read.

## Product Purpose

Alessandro Diaz is a Full-Stack Developer and CTO (NOUS, Costa Rica) who also consults on AI-driven process automation for enterprise clients (AuraQuantic). This portfolio is his primary professional introduction: the surface that decides whether a conversation happens at all.

Success means a qualified hiring manager or client reads the portfolio and reaches out because they feel they already understand who Alessandro is, what he builds, and what working with him would be like — before they've asked a single question.

The portfolio covers: primary hire-me landing, selected project case studies, work experience timeline, technical writing (blog), and a contact surface. It is bilingual (EN/ES).

## Brand Personality

**Sharp, restrained, authoritative.**

The feeling of something built to specification. A person in the room who doesn't need to speak first because the work speaks for them. Not terse for the sake of difficulty; direct because precision is a form of respect.

Voice: first-person, specific, anti-buzzword. Claims that can be backed by the project section below them. No "passionate about technology." No "innovative problem-solver." State the thing, then prove it.

## Anti-references

- **Agency over-art-direction:** Huge oblique serifs, liquid glass, 12-second load animations, Awwwards-shaped. Design that screams "notice the design" is the wrong signal for a developer portfolio. The work is the product, not the scroll effect.
- **Resume-as-a-website:** Timeline-first, skills-as-progress-bars, profile photo in a circle, LinkedIn structure. A portfolio that looks like an exported resume signals that the person hasn't thought about what they're trying to say.
- **The 'I love code' hacker aesthetic:** Terminal windows, green-on-black as identity, ASCII art as personality substitute, neon glow on everything. The hacker aesthetic signals "I like the idea of being a developer," not "I build systems that hold in production."

## Design Principles

1. **Show the judgment, not just the output.** Project descriptions must reveal why decisions were made, not only what was built. A visitor who can't tell whether Alessandro made the architecture call or was handed it cannot evaluate him. Every project should have a problem, a decision, and a consequence.

2. **Restraint as signal, not as safety.** Every element on screen earns its presence. The portfolio is quiet because it has nothing to prove — not because there was nothing to say. When an element is added, the reason is specific. "It looked good" is not a reason.

3. **Dual audience, one read.** Technical leaders scan for stack depth and judgment. Founders and PMs scan for communication clarity and reliability. Neither audience should have to read past what the other cares about to find what they need. The information architecture serves both simultaneously.

4. **Direct over clever.** The copy says exactly what it means. No metaphors that require unpacking. No aphorisms that sound meaningful but land empty. The headline "I build systems that hold" is a claim with a definition — not a vibe.

5. **Systems-thinking visible in the structure.** The portfolio's own architecture should reflect how Alessandro thinks: clear hierarchy, no orphaned sections, each piece connected to what follows it. A visitor should feel they understand the person before they understand the projects.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- **WCAG AA** minimum throughout; WCAG AAA target for body copy contrast ratios.
- `prefers-reduced-motion` respected for every animation — the content is always visible without motion; motion enhances but never gates.
- No color-only status indicators.
- Full keyboard navigation for all interactive elements (contact form, mobile menu, video controls, nav).
- Bilingual (EN/ES): content parity between languages; no language is a degraded version of the other.
- Skip-to-main-content link present for screen reader / keyboard users.
